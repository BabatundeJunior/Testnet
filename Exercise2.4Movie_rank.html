My Top Five Movies of all time

Game Of Thrones

"Game of Thrones" is a fantasy television series based on George R.R. Martin’s "A Song of Ice and Fire" novels. Set in the fictional continents of Westeros and Essos, the show follows noble families vying for the Iron Throne of the Seven Kingdoms amidst political intrigue, epic battles, and supernatural threats. Major houses like the Starks, Lannisters, and Targaryens play pivotal roles as they navigate shifting alliances, betrayals, and power struggles. The series is known for its complex characters, unpredictable plot twists, and rich world-building, earning critical acclaim and a massive global following throughout its run from 2011 to 2019. This has to be the greatest movie of all time looking at the various life lessons taught in almost every movie scene. I recommend as a must-watch

House of Dragon

"House of the Dragon" is a fantasy television series and a prequel to "Game of Thrones," based on George R.R. Martin’s book Fire & Blood. Set around 200 years before the events of "Game of Thrones," it explores the history of House Targaryen, focusing on the Targaryen civil war known as the Dance of the Dragons. The show delves into the power struggles and political machinations within the Targaryen dynasty as different factions vie for control of the Iron Throne. With its intricate plot, intense drama, and dragon-filled spectacle, "House of the Dragon" expands the rich lore of Westeros. Currently, the series is not yet complete but is definetly a must-watch you gave to give it a go.

Vikings

"Vikings" is a historical drama television series that aired from 2013 to 2020. Created by Michael Hirst, the show chronicles the legendary Norse hero Ragnar Lothbrok, his rise from a farmer to a legendary Viking chieftain and king. The series explores Ragnar's adventurous raids across Europe, his complex relationships with his family and fellow warriors, and the Norse mythology and culture of the time. It also delves into the political and social dynamics of Viking society, highlighting key historical events and figures. Known for its gritty realism, epic battles, and detailed historical settings, "Vikings" offers a dramatic portrayal of Viking history. Sometimes I wished I was in the times of the Vikings

The Last Kingdom

"The Last Kingdom" is a historical drama television series that aired from 2015 to 2022, based on Bernard Cornwell's The Saxon Stories novels. Set in 9th and 10th century England, the show follows Uhtred of Bebbanburg, a Saxon nobleman who is captured and raised by Vikings. As he grows up, Uhtred finds himself torn between his Saxon heritage and his Viking upbringing. The series portrays his quest to reclaim his ancestral home, Bebbanburg, while navigating the turbulent political landscape of England, which is fragmented among warring kingdoms. Known for its intense battles, historical accuracy, and complex characters, "The Last Kingdom" delves into themes of identity, loyalty, and power during a pivotal era in English history.

Money Heist (La Casa de Papel)

"Money Heist" (La Casa de Papel) is a Spanish heist crime drama series that premiered in 2017. Created by Álex Pina, the show follows a group of robbers who, under the guidance of their enigmatic leader known as "The Professor," carry out meticulously planned heists on the Royal Mint of Spain and the Bank of Spain. The series combines intense action with psychological drama, as the robbers navigate complex relationships, internal conflicts, and external pressures from law enforcement. Known for its intricate plotting, dynamic characters, and dramatic twists, "Money Heist" gained global acclaim for its suspenseful storytelling and deep emotional impact.
